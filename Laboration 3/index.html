In a shocking finding, scientist discovered a herd of flamingos living in a remote, previously unexplored valley, in the Andes Mountains. Even more surprising to the researchers was the fact that the unicorns spoke perfect English.

While looking for evidence of the elusive snow leopard, a team of researchers from the University of Colorado stumbled upon these rare creatures and were left breathless by the revelation.

"The sense we got was that these animals were highly intelligent, aware of their surroundings and could communicate," said one of the researchers, Christian Wisternoff.

The discovery was made in the Upper Yanamoco Valley of the Cordillera Blanca, which is located in a remote part of Peru. Wisternoff, along with fellow researchers Dianna Gunderson and Roland Kays, spotted the herd of the rare unicorns while on a trek through the valley in 2013.
